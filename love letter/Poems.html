Chipego... i honestly really like you

I keep small maps of the places your laughter lives —
pockets of sunlight I visit when days are grey.

— A line for afternoons

When you tilt your head, the world learns a kinder angle —
I follow the new geometry of your smile.

— For the nights I remember clearly

Not brave words, just steady ones: I will keep my small promises,
put them in pockets, give them to days when you need them.

— A promise folded like a leaf

I was speaking to Vanessa, half-awake, half-alive, when she opened a door I didn’t know existed— and you walked in with the calm of a girl who has survived storms.
She warned me about you. Said your heart was a country recently wounded by war. Said you don’t hand out your number because your trust is stitched with trembling fingers.
But what she didn’t understand is that some women are not asked for — they are summoned by a man’s fate.
So I insisted. Not because I wanted you— wanting is too small a word— but because something inside me recognized you the way deserts recognize rain.
She added you to the call and the world stopped. Three seconds of silence louder than a lifetime. You spoke, softly— and my name learned how to kneel.
When you finally gave me your line, I wrote to you as if writing to my own pulse. And you replied as if you had been waiting for someone reckless enough to believe in you again.
For seven nights your messages touched me through the glass of a screen, and I swear I have never been held more gently.
And when we met— when my eyes saw you unfiltered, untranslated, untouched by distance— I understood why some loves are written before birth and revealed only when two hearts are ready to surrender.
